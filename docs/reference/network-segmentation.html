The one idea to hold onto

Your defenses live in three layers — VLANs are the smallest.

Remote access and workload rules are already handled by the mesh and by Kubernetes. A VLAN only earns its keep for one job: stopping a hacked smart bulb from reaching your laptop — and that job is blocked by your Wi-Fi AP today. So: fix the real exposure cheaply, don't over-build VLANs.

Why VLANs are the small layer

Three layers do the guarding

Each layer stops a different kind of attacker. Two of them are already decided or live — which is why a big VLAN build-out mostly duplicates work you've already done.

Layer 1 · reach from outside

Mesh & firewall

Stops strangers reaching your stuff remotely. Headscale ACLs + Route10 rules on advertised routes.
DECIDED — "Tailscale-native, no client VLAN."
Layer 2 · reach inside cluster

Kubernetes NetworkPolicy

Stops a workload reaching your LAN. Default-deny; qBittorrent's egress excludes all private IPs.
LIVE — torrent pod already can't touch RFC1918.
Layer 3 · reach on the wire

L2 VLAN

The only layer that stops a hacked bulb reaching your laptop or server.
BLOCKED — your single-SSID AP can't tag the bulbs. Fix the AP, or this layer does nothing.
The plan, ranked by payoff

Do this, in order

Highest impact for least effort first. The last two are deliberately not to-dos.

1

Firewall the k3s API (ufw on opi5pro)

Allow :6443 only from you + the tailnet. It's the biggest local exposure and needs zero rewiring — and you already run ufw on that host.

effort · lowimpact · high
2

A Management VLAN

Put the router / switch / AP admin pages on a locked segment. Extra-justified now: Route10 is about to bridge the mesh into your LAN, so it's security-critical.

effort · lowimpact · med
3

A VLAN-capable Access Point (Alta)

The gate for any real bulb isolation. One SSID → IoT VLAN, same controller you already use. Everything IoT waits on this — including a clean HomeAssistant split later.

effort · medimpact · high (IoT)
4

A separate Servers VLAN for opi5pro

Only a modest gain over the ufw rule, and it collides with the edge plan's hard-wired 192.168.10.240. If you ever do it, pick the subnet before the edge cluster bakes it in.

effort · highimpact · low now
5

A dedicated VLAN for your own devices

Already replaced by Tailscale-native segmentation in the edge plan. Building it would just duplicate the mesh layer.

skip it
What changed from my earlier (text-heavy) answer
  • VPN = Headscale, not NetBird — and the "tunnel" is mesh-as-transport reverse ingress through the edge cluster. No Cloudflare Tunnel; Cloudflare gets retired entirely.
  • Torrent question is closed: classic qBittorrent, no egress VPN for now, contained by a NetworkPolicy that blocks all private IPs. Nothing more to decide there.
  • DNS is decided: split-horizon — AdGuard is the LAN "inside" view (keeps its MetalLB IP), edge PowerDNS is authoritative. A DNS backup was consciously declined.
  • MetalLB stays — it's how AdGuard & ingress get stable LAN IPs. It's the LAN door; the mesh is the internet door. Not in conflict.
